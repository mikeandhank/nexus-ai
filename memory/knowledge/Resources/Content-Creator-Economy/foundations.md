# Content & Creator Economy — Foundations

## Core Definition

The creator economy is the ecosystem where individuals create, curate, and distribute content to build audiences and monetize directly. Attention is the asset. Trust is the moat. The business model is downstream of the audience.

---

## Core Business Models

| Model | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Ads** | Platform revenue share (YouTube, TikTok) |
| **Sponsorships** | Brand deals, product placements |
| **Subscriptions** | Patreon, Substack, membership |
| **Merchandise** | Physical/digital product sales |
| **Affiliate** | Commission on referrals |
| **Direct fan funding** | Tips, Superchats, donations |

---

## Key Principles

1. **Audience first** — Build audience before monetization
2. **Trust is moat** — Attention is easy, trust is hard
3. **Owned relationships** — Reduce platform dependency
4. **Diversify revenue** — Don't rely on single source
5. **Consistency wins** — Show up, build habit

---

## Creator Tiers

| Tier | Revenue | Characteristics |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| Micro | <$50K | Niche, authentic |
| Mid | $50K-500K | Growing team |
| Macro | $500K-10M | Full business |
| Mega | $10M+ | Media company |

---

## Platform Dynamics

- Algorithm risk — Platform changes can destroy reach
- Multi-platform presence reduces single-point-of-failure
- Owned channels (newsletter, website) = true asset
