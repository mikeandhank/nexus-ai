# Content & Creator Economy — Case Studies

## 5 Successes

### 1. MrBeast

**What happened:** YouTube → $500M+ business.

**Core insight:**
- Content as product
- Merchandise as revenue
- Team as production company

**What it teaches:**
- Content can scale like product
- Off-platform revenue is stable
- Build a business, not a channel

---

### 2. Patreon

**What happened:** Creator subscription platform.

**Core insight:**
- Direct fan funding works
- Creators will pay for tools
- Platform enables individual businesses

**What it teaches:**
- Infrastructure for creators is valuable
- Subscriptions are stable revenue
- Tools enable the ecosystem

---

### 3. Substack

**What happened:** Newsletter platform for writers.

**Core insight:**
- Writers can monetize directly
- Email is owned relationship
- Subscription model works for creators

**What it teaches:**
- Owned channels beat platforms
- Writers are businesses too
- Simplicity wins

---

### 4. MrBeast Burger

**What happened:** Content → Consumer packaged goods.

**Core insight:**
- Audience can power product sales
- Physical products from content brand
- Multi-revenue streams

**What it teaches:**
- Content is marketing for product
- Brand can extend to physical goods
- Diversification reduces risk

---

### 5. Twitch Streamers

**What happened:** Live streaming to full-time income.

**Core insight:**
- Live = engagement = revenue
- Community is product
- Multiple revenue streams (subs, bits, ads)

**What it teaches:**
- Live creates commitment
- Community builds moat
- Diversification works

---

## 5 Failures

### 1. Creators Who Over-Monitized

**What happened:** Creators who sold out, lost audience.

**Fatal assumption:**
- Sponsorship money is guaranteed

**What went wrong:**
- Lost authenticity
- Audience trust destroyed
- Brand deals dried up

**What it teaches:**
- Trust is hard to rebuild
- Pacing matters

---

### 2. Platform Dependency

**What happened:** Creators who relied on single platform.

**Fatal assumption:**
- Platform will always support them

**What went wrong:**
- Algorithm changes
- Policy shifts
- Platform decline

**What it teaches:**
- Diversify platforms
- Own your audience

---

### 3. Newsletter Failures

**What happened:** Paid newsletters that lost subscribers.

**Fatal assumption:**
- People will pay for content

**What went wrong:**
- Not enough value
- Competition
- Unreliable delivery

**What it teaches:**
- Value must exceed cost
- Consistency matters

---

### 4. Merchandise Failures

**What happened:** Creator merchandise that didn't sell.

**Fatal assumption:**
- Audience will buy anything

**What went wrong:**
- Poor quality
- Wrong product
- No demand

**What it teaches:**
- Test before mass production
- Quality matters

---

### 5. Burnout

**What happened:** Creators who burned out.

**Fatal assumption:**
- Content creation is sustainable

**What went wrong:**
- Pace was unsustainable
- No team support
- Health failed

**What it teaches:**
- Build systems
- Hire help
- Pace yourself
