# Crypto & Onchain — Contrarian Views

## What Most People Get Wrong

### 1. "Tokens will appreciate"

**Wrong:** Buy tokens, hold, profit.

**Reality:**
- 96%+ of tokens launched in 2024-2025 failed
- 53.2% of tracked cryptocurrencies inactive by end-2025
- Token launches are frictionless (213K+ in 2022, 245K+ in 2023)
- Supply vastly outpaces demand; 86% of all historical failures occurred in 2025

**What works:** Real utility, fee revenue, value accrual to holders.

---

### 2. "Decentralization is always better"

**Wrong:** More decentralized = better.

**Reality:**
- Centralization offers speed, UX, security tradeoffs
- "Decentralization theater" — projects claim decentralization while retaining admin keys
- Practical systems use gradations: federated, consortium, on-chain with upgrades

**What works:** Pragmatic decentralization. Perfect is the enemy of good.

---

### 3. "Smart contracts are trustless"

**Wrong:** Code is law, can't be hacked.

**Reality:**
- Code has bugs: $2.7B stolen in 2025 (crypto's "darkest year")
- Oracles can fail (manipulation, data gaps)
- Upgradeability creates backdoors — who upgrades the contract?
- Centralization risk: single points of failure in infrastructure

**What works:** Code + governance + insurance + audits.

---

### 4. "Regulation will kill crypto"

**Wrong:** Regulations will destroy the industry.

**Reality (2024-2025 shift):**
- SEC Crypto Task Force launched Q2 2025
- Dismissed cases against Coinbase, Kraken, Ripple
- Stablecoin legislation advancing (licensing, reserve requirements)
- Institutional adoption follows regulatory clarity

**What works:** Engage regulators; compliance is a competitive advantage.

---

### 5. "DAOs will replace companies"

**Wrong:** DAOs are the future of organization.

**Reality:**
- Coordination is hard; proposals stall for months
- Low participation (<5% voter turnout typical)
- Whale dominance: top 10 holders control most votes
- Legal uncertainty: liability, taxation, employment

**What works:** DAO as coordination tool, not company substitute. Hybrid structures.

---

### 6. "L2s solve scalability"

**Wrong:** More L2s = more adoption.

**Reality:**
- L2 consolidation happening (Base, Arbitrum, Optimism dominate)
- Race to zero fees kills sustainability
- Kroma (2025) decommissioned — "just another L2" without unique value
- Cross-chain bridges become attack vectors

**What works:** Focus on specific use cases, not general scalability.

---

### 7. "CeFi is safer than DeFi"

**Wrong:** Centralized exchanges are more reliable.

**Reality:**
- FTX, Celsius, Three Arrows, Terra — CeFi failures
- Hot wallet hacks dominate thefts
- "Not your keys, not your coins" is still true

**What works:** Self-custody + insured custody; regulated exchanges with proof of reserves.

---

## Key Insights

| What Fails | What Works |
|------------|------------|
| Speculation without utility | Real revenue, value accrual |
| Dogmatic decentralization | Graduated, pragmatic approach |
| Code = trust | Code + governance + insurance |
| Fear regulation | Engage and comply |
| DAO = company | DAO = coordination layer |
| Launch and hope | Build liquidity, retain users |
| Infinite L2 competition | Winner-take-most consolidation |

## Uncomfortable Truths

- **Most crypto users don't care about decentralization** — They care about yield, convenience
- **Retail gets rekt** — Insiders, VCs, and sophisticated players extract value
- **MEV is a tax** — Max extractable value is a hidden cost borne by users
- **Marketing > product** — Many projects raise on narrative, not metrics
- **Liquidity is ephemeral** — Incentives end, LPs leave
