# Crypto & Onchain — Open Questions

## Known Unknowns

### 1. How will US regulatory framework evolve?

**Why I can't answer:**
- SEC Crypto Task Force (2025) is creating new rules but full framework not finalized
- Jurisdiction: SEC vs CFTC division still uncertain
- Stablecoin legislation progressing but not law
- International coordination unclear

**Specific uncertainties:**
- How will "security" vs "commodity" be defined for tokens?
- Will DeFi protocols be required to register?
- What custody rules emerge for institutions?

**What would change my mind:**
- Final SEC rules on custody and issuance (expected 2026)
- Stablecoin legislation passage
- Court decisions on pending cases

---

### 2. Can tokenized RWAs achieve real-world adoption?

**Why I can't answer:**
- Legal infrastructure not fully developed
- Custody and settlement questions
- Liquidity remains fragmented
- Regulatory clarity needed

**Specific uncertainties:**
- Will traditional finance adopt onchain settlement?
- How do securities laws apply to tokenized assets?
- Can real estate, private credit, equities tokenize at scale?

**What would change my mind:**
- Major bank/asset manager launches tokenized product at scale
- Legal framework clarity (SEC guidance on RWA securities)
- Institutional-grade custody solutions

---

### 3. What is the role of AI agents in crypto?

**Why I can't answer:**
- AI agents are new (2024-2025 emergence)
- Use cases still speculative
- Governance implications unclear
- Regulatory status uncertain

**Specific uncertainties:**
- Will AI agents become major onchain participants?
- Can AI improve or attack DeFi?
- Governance: can AI vote in DAOs legitimately?

**What would change my mind:**
- AI agents executing significant volume/TVL
- Proven AI trading strategies (sustainable alpha)
- Regulatory guidance on AI-generated transactions

---

### 4. Will crypto business models sustain without token incentives?

**Why I can't answer:**
- Current models heavily rely on token incentives for growth
- User acquisition costs in crypto are high
- Network effects require initial liquidity

**Specific uncertainties:**
- Can protocols grow organically (no airdrops, no points)?
- Will revenue-based models fund user acquisition?
- What替代 exists for incentive-driven flywheels?

**What would change my mind:**
- Documented case studies of sustainable organic growth
- Reduced reliance on token speculation for demand
- Clearer evidence that fees > incentive costs
