# Crypto & Onchain — Foundations

## Core Definition

Crypto and onchain businesses use blockchain technology to create decentralized, trustless systems. The fundamental insight: **token design is incentive design**. Most tokenomics fail because they assume users behave rationally — they don't.

---

## Business Models

### Layer 1 (L1) Networks
- **What:** Base blockchains (Ethereum, Solana, Avalanche)
- **Revenue:** Gas fees, staking, MEV
- **Moat:** Network effects, developer ecosystem, security
- **Key insight:** Platform plays are winner-take-most

### Layer 2 (L2) & Scaling
- **What:** Rollups, sidechains (Base, Arbitrum, Optimism)
- **Revenue:** Transaction fees, sequencing
- **Key insight:** Race to zero fees drives consolidation

### DeFi Protocols
- **Lending:** Aave, Compound — interest spread
- **DEXs:** Uniswap, Jupiter — swap fees
- **Perpetuals:** Hyperliquid, GMX — trading fees/spreads
- **Liquid Staking:** Jito, Lido — staking rewards
- **Key insight:** Fee revenue is the only sustainable model

### Infrastructure
- **Oracles:** Chainlink — data feeds
- ** Bridges:** Wormhole, Axelar — cross-chain
- **Custody:** Fireblocks, Coinbase Custody
- **Key insight:** Backend infrastructure captures steady value

### NFT & Gaming
- **Marketplaces:** OpenSea, Blur — trading fees
- **Gaming:** Immutable, Horizon — in-game purchases
- **Key insight:** Network effects in marketplaces

### Token Launch Platforms
- **What:** Pump.fun, Raydium — token creation
- **Revenue:** Trading fees without holding inventory
- **Key insight:** Aggregator model — take rate on volume

## Revenue Mechanics

| Model | Revenue Stream | Sustainability |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| **Fee-based** | Swap fees, lending interest, gas | High — real demand |
| **Staking/MEV** | Validator rewards, max extractable value | Medium — depends on chain |
| **Token appreciation** | Price gains | Low — speculative |
| **Value accrual** | Buybacks, burns, fee redistribution | Growing — aligns holders |
| **Licensing** | API access, enterprise | Emerging |

**2024-2025 Reality:** ~$16B in protocol revenue (2025), with 58% going to LPs. Sustainable protocols earn hard fees, not TVL.

## Tokenomics Fundamentals

### Value Accrual Mechanisms
1. **Buyback & burn** — Protocol uses revenue to purchase token, remove from supply
2. **Fee redistribution** — Holders earn share of protocol fees (Uniswap fee switch)
3. **Staking rewards** — Token locked for yield, reducing float
4. **Rebasing** — Supply adjusts dynamically

### Incentive Design Pitfalls
- **Inflationary dumps** — Token supply grows faster than demand
- **Vesting cliffs** — Large insider unlocks crush price
- **Mercenary capital** — Liquidity providers flee after incentives end
- **Governance capture** — Whales dominate voting

### Key Metrics
- **FDV (Fully Diluted Valuation)** — Total supply × price
- **TVL ratio** — TVL / FDV indicates over/undervaluation
- **Circulating supply** — What actually trades
- **Revenue/FDV** — Real yield metric

---

## Key Principles

1. **Token design = incentive design** — Users optimize for personal gain, not protocol health
2. **Fees > TVL** — Revenue is the only truth; TVL can be gamed with incentives
3. **Platform effects are everything** — Networks win; standalone protocols are vulnerable
4. **Security is table stakes** — $2.7B stolen in 2025; one hack kills projects
5. **Regulatory clarity is coming** — SEC Crypto Task Force (2025) creating frameworks
6. **Liquidity is a feature** — Not "获取," but something to build and maintain
