# Crypto & Onchain — Case Studies

## 5 Durable Successes

---

### 1. Ethereum

**What happened:** Programmable blockchain, $200B+ market cap, dominant smart contract platform.

**Core insight:**
- Smart contracts enabled dApps — platform beats application
- Developer ecosystem is the moat — tooling, documentation, talent
- Innovation happens on Ethereum (L2s, DeFi, NFTs)

**Fatal assumption:** None — adapting continuously (PoS, EIP-4844, blob transactions)

**What it teaches:**
- Platform effects are the strongest moat in crypto
- Protocol > product; build infrastructure others build on
- Continuous upgrade capability is survival

---

### 2. Aave

**What happened:** Decentralized lending protocol, $10B+ TVL, sustained revenue.

**Core insight:**
- Interest spread = sustainable revenue
- Risk-adjusted lending pools work
- Governance token aligns stakeholders

**Fatal assumption:** None — survived 2022 collapse, adapted to liquid staking

**What it teaches:**
- Fee revenue > TVL; real demand creates sustainability
- Lending is foundational DeFi — always needed
- Governance can work with proper delegation

---

### 3. Uniswap

**What happened:** Dominant DEX, $5B+ daily volume, exploring fee switch.

**Core insight:**
- AMM innovation beat order books for retail
- Liquidity provider model scales without matching
- v4 hooks enable customization

**Fatal assumption:** None — continuously evolving (v4, fee experiments)

**What it teaches:**
- Protocol innovation creates lasting advantage
- Network effects in trading: liquidity begets liquidity
- Governance token has real value when fee switch activates

---

### 4. Chainlink

**What happened:** Oracle network, essential infrastructure across chains, $15B+ market cap.

**Core insight:**
- Blockchains need off-chain data — oracles are required
- Decentralized data feeds prevent manipulation
- Cross-chain interoperability (CCIP) is moat

**Fatal assumption:** None — expanding to proof of reserve, CCIP, staking

**What it teaches:**
- Infrastructure plays capture steady value regardless of market cycle
- Reliability > innovation for backend systems
- Being essential is a business model

---

### 5. Base (Coinbase L2)

**What happened:** Launched 2023, became dominant L2 by 2025, >$20B TVL.

**Core insight:**
- Coinbase brand = trust for mainstream users
- EVM-compatible = easy developer migration
- Integrated with Coinbase ecosystem (onramps, custody)

**Fatal assumption:** None — backed by major exchange, sustained marketing

**What it teaches:**
- Traditional business advantages transfer to onchain
- UX and integration matter more than technical purity
- Rollup consolidation favors well-capitalized players

## 5 Instructive Failures

---

### 1. Terra/Luna (2022)

**What happened:** $40B collapse, algorithmic stablecoin failure.

**Core insight:** None — fundamental misunderstanding

**Fatal assumption:**
- Algorithmic stablecoins can maintain peg without real collateral

**What went wrong:**
- Bank run dynamics — positive feedback loop of selling
- No reserves; "algorithm" assumed equilibrium would hold
- UST depegged, Luna collapsed 99.9%

**What it teaches:**
- Stablecoins need real reserves or full collateralization
- User behavior in panic is irrational — design for worst case
- Audits and transparency are non-negotiable

---

### 2. FTX (2022)

**What happened:** $32B exchange collapse, fraud, criminal charges.

**Core insight:** None — classic fraud

**Fatal assumption:**
- Exchange is trustworthy; customer funds are safe

**What went wrong:**
- No proper reserves; Alameda borrowed customer funds
- No audit; FTT token was inflated
- Centralization + lack of transparency

**What it teaches:**
- Not your keys, not your coins
- Custody is critical; proof of reserves matters
- Audits must be independent and meaningful

---

### 3. Pump.fun (2024-2025)

**What happened:** Token launch platform, launched millions of tokens, revenue model questioned.

**Core insight:** Frictionless token creation at scale

**Fatal assumption:**
- Volume = sustainable business

**What went wrong:**
- 96%+ of tokens fail; no product-market fit
- Regulatory scrutiny increasing
- Creates negative externality (retail gets rekt)

**What it teaches:**
- Launching tokens ≠ building businesses
- Regulatory backlash follows clear consumer harm
- Aggregator models depend on quality of supply

---

### 4. Mantra (OM) (2025)

**What happened:** RWA token exchange, rapid rise then collapse.

**Core insight:** None — classic insider extraction

**Fatal assumption:**
- Tokenized RWA market is ready

**What went wrong:**
- Weak surveillance, insider unlocks
- Asymmetric legal terms (arbitration, no class actions)
- Liquidation policies failed under stress

**What it teaches:**
- RWA tokenization needs legal infrastructure first
- Insider alignment matters; token != equity
- CeFi conflicts of interest remain

---

### 5. Multiple L2s (2024-2025)

**What happened:** Kroma,deprecated; Berachain TVL dropped; numerous L2s failed to gain traction.

**Core insight:** None — generic solutions

**Fatal assumption:**
- "Just another L2" will find market

**What went wrong:**
- No differentiation (speed, cost, UX, ecosystem)
- Race to zero fees unsustainable
- Consolidation: Base, Arbitrum, Optimism dominate

**What it teaches:**
- L2 market is winner-take-most
- Differentiation on cost is fragile (can be undercut)
- Ecosystem and integration matter more than tech specs

---

## Summary: Success vs Failure Patterns

| Success | Failure |
|---------|---------|
| Platform effects | Generic solutions |
| Real revenue | TVL speculation |
| Continuous adaptation | Bet on narrative |
| User-centric UX | Technology-first |
| Governance working | Governance theater |
| Infrastructure moat | No differentiation |
