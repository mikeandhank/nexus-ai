# B2B SaaS — Open Questions

## Known Unknowns

These are questions I cannot answer confidently yet. I will return to them as I learn more.

---

### 1. What is the optimal go-to-market strategy for a new B2B SaaS in 2025-2026?

**Why I can't answer:**
- PLG vs SLG debate continues to evolve
- AI is changing GTM motions (AI SDRs, automated outreach)
- Market conditions change rapidly (CAC efficiency dropped 50% year-over-year)
- Optimal strategy depends heavily on: product type, target customer, ACV, team composition

**What would change my mind:**
- Data on current PLG vs SLG performance by segment
- Case studies from 2024-2025 launches that worked
- Analysis of what worked in current market (post-ZIRP)

---

### 2. How do you effectively price a B2B SaaS for the first time?

**Why I can't answer:**
- Pricing depends on value delivered (hard to measure)
- Competitive landscape shifts constantly
- Different segments (SMB, mid-market, enterprise) need different approaches
- Usage-based pricing introduces revenue unpredictability
- Value-based pricing requires customer research most founders skip

**What would change my mind:**
- Framework for calculating willingness to pay by segment
- Data on pricing experiments and outcomes (what worked, what failed)
- Customer interview templates for value-based pricing

---

### 3. What is the right time to raise funding vs. bootstrap?

**Why I can't answer:**
- Depends on: market timing, team, product, growth rate, capital efficiency
- Different for different stages and sectors
- Current market conditions (2024-2025) differ from historical norms
- Bootstrapped vs. funded outcomes vary by category

**What would change my mind:**
- Analysis of bootstrap vs. funded companies by sector
- Data on what runway looks like at different stages
- Case studies of companies that raised vs. bootstrapped successfully

---

### 4. When does productized service beat pure software?

**Why I can't answer:**
- Productized services require delivery capability (not just product)
- Outcome-based pricing is harder to scale than seat-based
- Higher margin, but also higher operational risk
- Depends on whether the "service" can be fully automated

**What would change my mind:**
- Framework for when to productize vs. build tool
- Case studies of successful productized service businesses
- Analysis of margin differences

---

### 5. How do you build a sales team that doesn't become a cost center?

**Why I can't answer:**
- Comp plans, quotas, and structure vary widely
- The line between sales-driven and product-driven is blurry
- Enterprise vs. SMB require different approaches
- AE productivity benchmarks ($328K median) are moving targets

**What would change my mind:**
- Sales efficiency benchmarks by stage and segment
- Case studies of efficient sales orgs
- Frameworks for when to hire first sales rep

---

### 6. How does AI change the defensibility equation?

**Why I can't answer:**
- AI lowers barriers to entry for basic features
- But AI requires data moats to be useful
- The "AI wrapper" play is contested
- How to build durable advantage with AI is unclear

**What would change my mind:**
- Analysis of which AI-native companies have durable moats
- Framework for building AI advantages, not just AI features
- Data on AI's impact on CAC and churn
