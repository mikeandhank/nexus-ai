# B2B SaaS — Contrarian Views

## What Most People Get Wrong

### 1. "Build features, they will come"

**Wrong:** More features = more value = more customers.

**Reality:** Value comes from workflow integration, not feature count. Churn is a product problem that shows up in revenue. The deeper you sit in a customer's workflow, the higher retention. Adding features without clear workflow integration creates bloat that increases churn.

### 2. "Move fast and break things"

**Wrong:** Speed is the primary variable. Move fast.

**Reality:** The question is not "how fast can we move" but "how fast can we move without creating problems that slow us down more later." Technical debt compounds. Move fast in ways that are reversible. Move carefully in ways that are not.

### 3. "Sales fixes churn"

**Wrong:** If customers leave, hire more salespeople or improve sales training.

**Reality:** Churn is a product problem. Sales can temporarily hold customers, but without product value, they'll leave. Fix the product, don't paper over it with sales.

### 4. "PLG is always better than SLG"

**Wrong:** Product-led is the modern way. Sales-led is outdated.

**Reality:** Most successful B2B SaaS companies are hybrids. The right model depends on ACV, sales cycle complexity, and product complexity. Pure PLG works for simple tools. Complex enterprise deals need sales. The median CAC ratio dropped to 3.19 in 2025 — efficiency matters more than channel.

### 5. "Freemium is always good"

**Wrong:** Free tier builds audience, converts to paid.

**Reality:** Freemium can work, but:
- Not all products benefit from it
- Free users cost money to support
- Conversion rates are often <5%
- Can train users not to pay

### 6. "Pricing is secondary"

**Wrong:** Get product-market fit first, then figure out pricing.

**Reality:** Pricing is part of PMF. Wrong pricing:
- Leaves money on the table (too low)
- Blocks adoption (too high)
- Signals value incorrectly

### 7. "The market decides"

**Wrong:** Build a great product and the market will adopt it.

**Reality:** Distribution is the business. Most failures are distribution failures dressed up as product failures. Product is necessary but not sufficient.

### 8. "Technical founders have an advantage"

**Wrong:** Non-technical founders can hire CTOs.

**Reality:** Technical founders survive 3-5x better than non-technical. The hidden costs of non-technical leadership are massive — poor technical decisions, inability to evaluate talent, misalignment with build decisions.

### 9. "18-month rule is outdated"

**Wrong:** With more capital and better tools, the timeline has changed.

**Reality:** Seed funding lasts 12-18 months. PMF typically takes 18 months to find. 92% of SaaS startups fail within 3 years, with 45% failing in the 18-24 month window. The math hasn't changed.

### 10. "Growth at all costs"

**Wrong:** Scale fast, figure out profitability later.

**Reality:** Burn multiple matters. Target <2.0. Beyond $10M ARR, >3.0 burn multiple signals inefficiency. Growth without unit economics is just burning capital faster. The median ARR growth dropped to 26% in 2025 — efficient growth beats fast growth.

### 11. "AI changes everything"

**Wrong:** AI will eliminate the need for traditional SaaS playbooks.

**Reality:** AI changes what you can build, not the fundamentals. Unit economics, churn, defensibility — all still matter. AI might lower CAC but also lowers barriers to entry. The winners will be those who use AI to deepen workflow integration, not those who simply add AI features.

### 12. "Vertical SaaS is smaller"

**Wrong:** Horizontal plays are bigger and more valuable.

**Reality:** Vertical SaaS often wins because:
- Deeper workflow integration
- Higher switching costs
- Less competition from well-funded horizontals
- Faster toPMF

## What Separates Durable Businesses from Impressive Collapses

1. **Unit economics before scaling** — Prove LTV:CAC before aggressive growth
2. **Workflow integration over features** — Deeper in workflow = higher retention
3. **Defensibility answered** — Know what stops competitors
4. **Governance and oversight** — WeWork failed due to board failure, not just Neumann
5. **Cash flow discipline** — 29% of failures from cash flow issues
6. **Team capability** — Solo founders fail 3x more; technical founders 3-5x better survival
7. **AI as multiplier, not strategy** — AI features ≠ defensible business

## The Uncomfortable Truths

- **Churn is fatal:** 5% monthly churn = ~46% annual churn. At that rate, you need to replace half your customers every year just to stay flat.
- **Expansion pays for acquisition:** The best SaaS businesses grow primarily through existing customers (NRR >100%). New logo acquisition is the hard part.
- **Productized services may beat pure software:** If you can deliver the outcome cheaper than humans, you have higher margins and less churn. But you also have delivery risk.
- **The median SaaS company is not profitable:** Median Rule of 40 is 15%. Most companies aren't hitting the benchmark they'd brag about.
