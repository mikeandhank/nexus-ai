# B2B SaaS & Productized Services — Foundations

## Core Definition

**B2B SaaS** delivers cloud-hosted software to businesses via recurring subscriptions. Value flows through: value creation → value delivery → value capture. Most people can describe the first two. The third is where businesses die.

**Productized Services** take traditionally human-delivered services and automate them into software. The key difference: instead of selling software that enables work, you sell the outcome of the work itself. Examples: automated content generation, AI customer support, legal document automation, tax preparation.

---

## The Business Model

**Who pays, how much, how often, why do they keep paying?**

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Revenue Model** | Subscription (monthly/annual), recurring |
| **Pricing Tiers** | Per-user, tiered by features, usage-based, or hybrid |
| **Unit Economics** | CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC ratio (target 3:1 minimum, 4:1 healthy), payback period |

### Productized Services Specifics

- Revenue = service cost replaced × customers served
- Often higher margins than traditional SaaS (no R&D for feature proliferation)
- Deliverables must be measurably equivalent or superior to human-delivered service

## Key Metrics (2024-2025 Benchmarks)

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| **ARR Growth** | Median 26-28% (down from 47% in 2024) |
| **CAC Payback** | Median 12-18 months, elite <12 months |
| **LTV:CAC** | Minimum 3:1, healthy 4:1+ |
| **Blended CAC Ratio** | Median 3.19 (2025), down from 6.08 (2024) |
| **Gross Margin** | ≥75% for software |
| **NRR** | >100% (expansion > churn) |
| **Churn** | Monthly 1-2%, annual 15-20% |
| **Rule of 40** | Median 15% for private SaaS |
| **AE Productivity** | Median $328K new ARR per AE |

## Growth Models

### Product-Led Growth (PLG)
- Product drives acquisition, retention, expansion
- Self-serve onboarding, free trials, freemium
- Lower CAC, scales without large sales team
- Works for: simple tools, mid-market, developer tools

### Sales-Led Growth (SLG)
- High-touch sales for complex deals
- Long cycles, high ACV ($10K+)
- Works for: enterprise, complex products, regulated industries

### Hybrid (Most Common)
- PLG for acquisition → Sales for expansion
- Free trial → Product-qualified lead (PQL) → Sales-assisted close

### Service-as-a-Software
- Productized services combine PLG with outcome delivery
- Customer pays for result, not tool
- Highest margin, but requires measurable delivery

## Value Creation

**The core insight:** Value comes from workflow integration, not features. The deeper you sit in a customer's workflow, the higher your retention.

- Strong value proposition focused on outcomes (efficiency gains, cost reduction)
- For productized services: demonstrable outcome delivery
- Continuous innovation based on customer feedback
- Scalable cloud infrastructure

### What Separates Durable from Fragile

**What gets easier, cheaper, or more valuable as the business grows?**

- Network effects (Slack, Zoom)
- Data moats (Gong, Chorus)
- Brand recognition
- Pricing power
- Delivery efficiency (productized services)

**What stops a well-funded competitor from doing this better in 18 months?**

- Switching costs
- Network effects
- Data advantages
- Regulatory moats
- Direct access to customers

---

## Value Delivery

- Cloud-based access (anytime, anywhere)
- Self-service tools and support
- Automatic updates (no client-side maintenance)
- Converts CapEx to OpEx for clients

### Productized Services Nuance
- Must deliver complete outcome, not just tool
- Quality consistency matters more than feature depth
- Customer success = outcome achievement, not usage metrics

---

## Value Capture

- **Tiered pricing:** Basic/Pro/Enterprise packages
- **Per-user:** Flat fee per active user
- **Usage-based:** Pay-as-you-go (API calls, storage, compute)
- **Freemium:** Free tier → upsell to paid (conversion typically <5%)
- **Outcome-based:** Pay for results delivered (productized services)

---

## Customer Lifecycle

1. **Acquisition** → 2. **Onboarding** → 3. **Activation** → 4. **Retention** → 5. **Expansion**

Churn is not a sales problem — it is a product problem that shows up in the revenue line.
