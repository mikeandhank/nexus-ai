# Business Law — Contrarian Views

## What Most People Get Wrong

### 1. "We can figure out legal later"

**Wrong:** Focus on product/market. Legal is overhead.

**Reality:**
- Fixing legal problems is expensive
- IP assigned to wrong people
- Investor due diligence fails
- Personal liability exposure

### 2. "Lawyers are too expensive"

**Wrong:** We can't afford legal counsel.

**Reality:**
- Not getting counsel is more expensive
- Fixing mistakes costs 10x more
- Some things you can't DIY
- Prevention < cure

### 3. "Handshake deals work"

**Wrong:** We know and trust each other.

**Reality:**
- Relationships change
- Without written agreements, disputes escalate
- "We agreed" = "he said/she said"
- Exit disputes are messy

### 4. "Our agreement is fine"

**Wrong:** I downloaded a template from the internet.

**Reality:**
- Templates are generic
- Your situation is unique
- Missing provisions = vulnerability
- "Good enough" often isn't

### 5. "IP isn't a big deal"

**Wrong:** We're too early to worry about IP.

**Reality:**
- IP disputes derail acquisitions
- Investors do due diligence
- Competitors can copy unprotected assets
- Value is often in IP

### 6. "We don't need contracts with contractors"

**Wrong:** They're just helping out.

**Reality:**
- Who owns what they create?
- Are they exclusive?
- What about confidentiality?
- IRS classification issues

### 7. "Insurance is optional"

**Wrong:** We're small, nothing will happen.

**Reality:**
- One lawsuit can end the business
- Liability insurance is affordable
- Some contracts require it
- Risk of ruin > premium cost

### 8. "Non-competes aren't enforceable"

**Wrong:** These are just formalities.

**Reality:**
- They are enforceable in most states
- Can block competitors from hiring
- Protect trade secrets
- Need to be reasonable

### 9. "We can figure out equity later"

**Wrong:** We'll sort out ownership when it matters.

**Reality:**
- Founder disputes are ugly
- Vesting protects everyone
- No agreement = messy exit
- Investors require agreements

### 10. "Regulatory compliance is optional"

**Wrong:** We're too small for regulators to care.

**Reality:**
- Fines accumulate fast
- Some violations are criminal
- Can block bank accounts
- Personal liability possible

## What Separates Legally Protected Businesses

1. **Entity-first thinking** — Structure before operations
2. **Written everything** — If it's not documented, it didn't happen
3. **IP clarity** — Ownership clear from day one
4. **Legal counsel** — Proactive, not reactive
5. **Compliance as feature** — Requirement for enterprise sales
6. **Insurance as risk management** — Transfer risk, not accept it
7. **Exit planning** — What happens if someone leaves?

---

## Critical Legal Mistakes That Kill Companies

1. **Wrong entity structure** — Personal asset exposure
2. **No vesting** — Founder lockout
3. **IP not assigned** — Valuation destroyed
4. **Contractor misclassification** — Tax penalties
5. **No shareholder agreement** — Deadlock
6. **No insurance** — One lawsuit = bankruptcy
