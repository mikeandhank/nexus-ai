# Business Law — Case Studies

## 5 Successes

### 1. Apple — Entity Evolution

**What happened:** From garage partnership to $4T corporation.

**Core insight:**
- Started as partnership (risky)
- Evolved to C-Corp for investor protection
- Clear ownership structure enabled funding
- Strong IP protection

**What it teaches:**
- Structure should evolve with stage
- C-Corp needed for VC funding
- Legal structure enables growth

---

### 2. Google — IP Strategy

**What happened:** Built patent portfolio that protected Android.

**Core insight:**
- Acquired patents strategically
- Defended against IP lawsuits
- Built defensive portfolio

**What it teaches:**
- Patents are assets
- IP protection enables monetization
- Defensive patents matter

---

### 3. Stripe — Contract Clarity

**What happened:** Built legal infrastructure for payments.

**Core insight:**
- Clear terms of service
- Regulatory compliance built in
- Developer agreements protect IP

**What it teaches:**
- Contract clarity builds trust
- Compliance enables scale
- Legal infrastructure is competitive advantage

---

### 4. Slack — Employee Equity

**What happened:** Clear vesting, survived founder transitions.

**Core insight:**
- 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff
- Clear IP assignment
- Well-documented equity

**What it teaches:**
- Equity clarity retains talent
- Vesting protects company
- Documentation prevents disputes

---

### 5. Basecamp — Governance

**What happened:** Survived controversy with strong governance.

**Core insight:**
- Clear decision-making structure
- Employee agreements in place
- Transparent policies

**What it teaches:**
- Governance matters in crisis
- Documentation protects
- Clear policies enable action

---

## 5 Failures

### 1. Uber — Misclassification

**What happened:** Drivers classified as contractors, massive legal battles.

**Fatal assumption:**
- Drivers are contractors, not employees

**What went wrong:**
- Classification lawsuits
- Back taxes and penalties
- Regulatory changes required

**What it teaches:**
- Worker classification matters
- Regulations can change
- Classification has costs

---

### 2. Theranos — No IP Validation

**What happened:** Technology didn't work, fraud exposed.

**Fatal assumption:**
- Could claim IP protection for non-existent tech

**What went wrong:**
- No actual IP to protect
- Fraud investigations
- Criminal charges

**What it teaches:**
- IP must be real
- Fraud destroys everything
- Due diligence will find truth

---

### 3. Zenefits — Licensing Violations

**What happened:** Skipped insurance licensing requirements.

**Fatal assumption:**
- Can operate without proper licensing

**What went wrong:**
- Regulatory penalties
- License issues in multiple states
- Settlement costs

**What it teaches:**
- Compliance is not optional
- Regulations exist for reason
- Violations have consequences

---

### 4. Example: No Founders Agreement

**What happened:** Co-founders dispute, company collapses.

**Fatal assumption:**
- We trust each other, agreement unnecessary

**What went wrong:**
- Disagreement over ownership
- No mechanism to resolve
- Company deadlocked

**What it teaches:**
- Trust is not enough
- Agreements prevent disputes
- Exit mechanisms matter

---

### 5. Example: IP Not Assigned

**What happened:** Employee leaves, claims code ownership.

**Fatal assumption:**
- Employee work belongs to company

**What went wrong:**
- No written assignment
- No IP clause in contract
- Costly litigation

**What it teaches:**
- IP must be assigned in writing
- Document everything
- Employment contracts matter

---

## Cross-Cutting Lessons

| Success Factors | Failure Factors |
|----------------|----------------|
| Proper entity | Wrong structure |
| Clear IP ownership | IP disputes |
| Written contracts | Handshake deals |
| Compliance built-in | Regulatory violations |
| Insurance | No risk transfer |
| Equity clarity | Founder disputes |

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Entity first** — Structure before operations
2. **Everything in writing** — Documentation protects
3. **IP clarity** — Who owns what, from day one
4. **Legal counsel** — Proactive not reactive
5. **Compliance** — Not optional, enables scale
6. **Insurance** — Transfer risk, not accept it
7. **Equity clarity** — Vesting prevents disputes
8. **Contractor classification** — Correctly classify
