# Business Law — Foundations

## Core Definition

Business law governs how businesses form, operate, and interact. It provides the structural framework that protects assets, defines relationships, and enables commerce.

---

## Entity Formation

### Business Structures

| Structure | Liability | Taxation | Best For |
|-----------|-----------|----------|----------|
| **Sole Proprietorship** | Unlimited personal | Pass-through | Small, low-risk |
| **Partnership** | Unlimited personal | Pass-through | Multiple owners |
| **LLC** | Limited | Pass-through | Small business, flexibility |
| **C-Corp** | Limited | Corporate | Scaling startup, VC funding |
| **S-Corp** | Limited | Pass-through | Small corp, tax savings |

### Why Structure Matters

- **Liability protection** — Shields personal assets
- **Investor requirements** — VCs require C-Corp
- **Tax implications** — Different entities = different tax treatment
- **Ability to raise capital** — Stock options require Corp

## Contracts

### Essential Contracts

| Contract | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| **Founders Agreement** | Ownership, vesting, roles |
| **Employment Contract** | Role, compensation, IP assignment |
| **NDA (Non-Disclosure)** | Protect confidential info |
| **NCA (Non-Compete)** | Restrict post-employment competition |
| **Operating Agreement** | LLC rules, profit distribution |
| **Shareholders Agreement** | Corp rules, exit procedures |
| **Investor Agreement** | Terms, rights, preferences |
| **Client/Service Agreement** | Scope, payment, liability |

### Contract Elements

1. **Offer** — Clear proposal
2. **Acceptance** — Unqualified agreement
3. **Consideration** — Value exchanged
4. **Capacity** — Legal ability to contract
5. **Legality** — Lawful purpose

## Intellectual Property (IP)

### Types of IP

| Type | Protection | Duration |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Patent** | Inventions | 20 years |
| **Trademark** | Brand names, logos | Indefinite (renewable) |
| **Copyright** | Creative works | Life + 70 years |
| **Trade Secret** | Confidential info | Indefinite (if secret) |

### IP Best Practices

- **Assignments** — Employees assign IP to company
- **NDAs** — Protect confidential information
- **Trademarks** — Register early, monitor infringement
- **Patents** — File before public disclosure
- **Due diligence** — Audit IP ownership before acquisition

## Liability

### Types of Liability

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Contractual** | Breach of contract |
| **Tort** | Negligence, harm to others |
| **Regulatory** | Violation of laws/regulations |
| **Personal** | Piercing the corporate veil |

### Limiting Liability

- Proper entity formation
- Adequate insurance
- Written contracts
- Compliance programs
- Separate personal/business finances

## Securities Law

### Funding and Securities

- Selling equity = securities offering
- Must comply with SEC regulations
- Exemptions: Regulation D, crowdfunding
- Required disclosures to investors

### Common Mistakes

- Not registering required offerings
- Misleading investors
- Ignoring filing requirements

## Employment Law

### Key Areas

- **Classification** — Employee vs. contractor (critical!)
- **Compensation** — Minimum wage, overtime
- **Discrimination** — Illegal to discriminate
- **IP Assignment** — Who owns what employee creates

### Contractor vs. Employee

| Factor | Employee | Contractor |
|--------|----------|------------|
| Control | Employer controls | Independent |
| Tools | Employer provides | Contractor provides |
| Hours | Employer sets | Contractor sets |
| Benefits | Required | Not required |
| Tax | W-2 | 1099 |

## Legal Framework for Decision-Making

Before any significant business decision:

1. **What are the legal risks?** (liability, regulatory)
2. **What contracts are needed?**
3. **Who owns the IP?**
4. **What compliance is required?**
5. **What's the worst legal outcome?**
6. **Is legal counsel needed?**

---

## Key Principles

1. **Structure first** — Get entity right from start
2. **Contracts in writing** — Handshake deals fail
3. **IP clarity** — Who owns what
4. **Comply early** — Fixing is harder than following
5. **Document everything** — If it's not written, it didn't happen
