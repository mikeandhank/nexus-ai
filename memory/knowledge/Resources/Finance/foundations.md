# Finance — Foundations

## Core Definition

Finance is the study of how money flows through a business — how it's raised, spent, managed, and grown. For a business operator, finance is the language of decisions.

---

## Core Accounting Concepts

| Element | Definition |
|---------|------------|
| **Assets** | Resources owned that generate future value |
| **Liabilities** | Money owed to creditors |
| **Equity** | Owner investment + retained earnings |
| **Revenue** | Income from sales/services |
| **Expenses** | Costs to generate revenue |
| **Profit** | Revenue - Expenses |

---

## The Accounting Equation

```
Assets = Liabilities + Equity
```

Every transaction must keep this equation balanced.

---

## Three Core Financial Statements

### 1. Income Statement (P&L)
- **Revenue** → **Expenses** → **Net Profit**
- Shows profitability over a period
- Key metrics: Gross profit, operating profit, net profit

### 2. Balance Sheet
- **Assets** = **Liabilities** + **Equity**
- Snapshot at a point in time
- Shows financial position

### 3. Cash Flow Statement
- Cash in - Cash out
- Operating / Investing / Financing activities
- Tracks actual cash movement

## Key Metrics for SaaS/Startup

### Revenue Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Formula |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **MRR** | Monthly Recurring Revenue | Customers × Avg price |
| **ARR** | Annual Recurring Revenue | MRR × 12 |
| **ARPU** | Avg Revenue Per User | MRR / Customers |

### Growth Metrics

| Metric | Definition |
|--------|------------|
| **MRR Growth** | Month-over-month revenue growth |
| **Net Revenue Retention (NRR)** | Revenue from existing customers (renewal + expansion) |
| **Gross Revenue Retention** | Without expansion, just renewal |

### Unit Economics

| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| **CAC** | (Sales + Marketing) / New customers | Minimize |
| **LTV** | ARPU × Customer lifetime | Maximize |
| **LTV:CAC** | LTV / CAC | 3:1 minimum |
| **Payback Period** | CAC / ARPU | <12 months |

### Health Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|------------|--------|
| **Churn** | % customers lost | <5% monthly |
| **Gross Margin** | Revenue - COGS | >70% |
| **Burn Rate** | Monthly cash spent | Minimize |
| **Runway** | Cash / Burn rate | 18+ months |

## Funding Stages and Benchmarks

| Stage | LTV:CAC | Payback | NRR | Churn |
|-------|---------|---------|-----|-------|
| Seed | 2-3x | 12-18 mo | ~100% | 15-20% |
| Series A | 3-4x | 9-12 mo | 100-110% | 10-15% |
| Series B+ | 4-5x+ | <9 mo | 110-125% | 5-10% |

## Accounting Methods

### Accrual Basis
- Record when transaction occurs, not when paid
- Standard for larger businesses
- Shows true profitability

### Cash Basis
- Record only when cash changes hands
- Simpler, used by small businesses
- Can miss true financial position

## Valuation Methods

| Method | Description | Best For |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Revenue Multiple** | Revenue × Multiple | SaaS, high growth |
| **EBITDA Multiple** | EBITDA × Multiple | Traditional business |
| **DCF** | Discounted cash flow | Stable cash flows |
| **Comparable** | Similar company multiples | Any |

## Financial Decision Framework

Before any financial decision:
1. **What's the ROI?** (Return on investment)
2. **What's the payback period?**
3. **How does it affect runway?**
4. **What's the opportunity cost?** (What else could this money do?)
5. **Is it reversible?**

---

## Key Principles

1. **Cash is oxygen** — Never run out
2. **Unit economics first** — Prove LTV:CAC before scaling
3. **Revenue validates** — Growth without revenue is just burning money
4. **Conservative forecasting** — Plan for worst case
5. **Know your numbers** — Metrics drive decisions
