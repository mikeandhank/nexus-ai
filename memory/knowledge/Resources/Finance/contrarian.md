# Finance — Contrarian Views

## What Most People Get Wrong

### 1. "Revenue solves everything"

**Wrong:** If we're growing revenue, we're doing fine.

**Reality:**
- Revenue ≠ profit
- Revenue ≠ cash flow
- Burning cash to generate revenue is not sustainable
- 29% of startups fail due to cash flow issues

### 2. "Profit equals cash"

**Wrong:** If the P&L shows profit, we have money.

**Reality:**
- Accrual accounting records revenue when earned, not received
- Profit on paper, bankrupt in reality
- Need cash flow statement to know actual position

### 3. "More funding = more success"

**Wrong:** Raise as much as possible, grow faster.

**Reality:**
- Dilution reduces founder ownership
- More capital = higher expectations
- "Burning money" mentality kills discipline
- Some funded companies fail faster than bootstrapped

### 4. "Growth at all costs"

**Wrong:** Prioritize growth. Profit comes later.

**Reality:**
- Unsustainable growth kills companies
- Unit economics matter at every stage
- 92% of SaaS startups fail — many from burn without path to profit

### 5. "VC is the only way"

**Wrong:** To scale, you need venture capital.

**Reality:**
- Bootstrapped companies survive better initially
- Revenue-based financing exists
- Customer funding is the purest validation
- Not every business needs to be venture-scale

### 6. "Expenses are bad"

**Wrong:** Cut costs to increase profit.

**Reality:**
- Some expenses are investments
- Under-investing slows growth
- The question is ROI, not expense level
- "Cheap" can be expensive if it limits capability

### 7. "Forecasts are predictions"

**Wrong:** Build a plan and follow it.

**Reality:**
- Forecasts are assumptions, not predictions
- Need best/base/worst scenarios
- Update frequently based on actuals
- Plan for uncertainty

### 8. "Bigger team = bigger results"

**Wrong:** Hire fast to scale faster.

**Reality:**
- Payroll is the biggest expense
- Bad hires cost 3-5x salary
- Over-hiring based on growth that didn't materialize = layoffs
- Efficiency > headcount

### 9. "Churn is a sales problem"

**Wrong:** If customers leave, fix sales.

**Reality:**
- Churn is a product problem
- 5% monthly churn = 50% annual churn
- Churn compounds — losing 10% is losing 40% over 5 years
- Retention > acquisition

### 10. "Pricing doesn't matter"

**Wrong:** Get product-market fit first. Optimize pricing later.

**Reality:**
- Pricing is part of PMF
- Wrong pricing kills: too high blocks adoption, too low leaves money on table
- Pricing signals value
- 10% price increase = often 100%+ profit increase

## Where Conventional Wisdom Fails

| Conventional Wisdom | Why It Fails |
|---------------------|--------------|
| Revenue solves everything | Revenue ≠ cash or profit |
| Profit = cash | Accrual vs. cash basis |
| More funding = success | Dilution, expectations |
| Growth at all costs | Unsustainable burn |
| VC is only way | Bootstrapping viable |
| Expenses are bad | Investment vs. cost |
| Forecasts are predictions | Assumptions, not facts |
| Bigger team = results | Payroll is biggest expense |
| Churn is sales | Product problem |
| Pricing can wait | Part of PMF |

## What Separates Financially Healthy Companies

1. **Unit economics discipline** — LTV:CAC 3:1+ before aggressive scaling
2. **Cash management** — Never surprise on runway
3. **Conservative forecasting** — Plan for adversity
4. **Pricing optimization** — Test and iterate
5. **Efficient growth** — Do more with less
6. **Profit focus** — Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity
7. **Financial visibility** — Real-time metrics, not month-end surprises
