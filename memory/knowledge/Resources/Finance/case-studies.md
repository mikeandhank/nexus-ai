# Finance — Case Studies

## 5 Successes

### 1. Amazon

**What happened:** From online bookstore to $1T+ company.

**Core insight:**
- Prioritized long-term growth over short-term profit
- Invested heavily in infrastructure (Prime, logistics)
- Reinvested all profits into growth

**What it teaches:**
- Long-term thinking beats quarterly performance
- Infrastructure investments pay off
- Customer obsession → revenue → loyalty → more revenue

---

### 2. Apple

**What happened:** From near-bankruptcy to $3T+ company.

**Core insight:**
- Premium pricing with premium product
- Ecosystem lock-in drives recurring revenue
- Services revenue (App Store, Apple Music) = high margin

**What it teaches:**
- Pricing power from brand and ecosystem
- Recurring revenue > one-time sales
- Margin matters more than volume

---

### 3. Stripe

**What happened:** Payments infrastructure worth $95B+.

**Core insight:**
- Developer-first approach
- Embedded in millions of businesses
- Revenue scales with GMV

**What it teaches:**
- Infrastructure businesses have high margins
- Embedding in workflows = defensibility
- Revenue scales without proportional cost increase

---

### 4. Bootstrapped Companies (Basecamp, Mailchimp, etc.)

**What happened:** Built profitable, independent companies without VC.

**Core insight:**
- Customer revenue validates product
- No pressure for hypergrowth
- Founder retains control

**What it teaches:**
- VC is optional
- Profit enables independence
- Slow growth can be sustainable growth

---

### 5. Salesforce

**What happened:** Pioneered SaaS subscription model.

**Core insight:**
- Recurring revenue = predictable cash flow
- Land and expand drives NRR
- No upfront software purchase

**What it teaches:**
- Subscription model transforms valuation
- Customer success drives expansion
- Recurring revenue is an asset

---

## 5 Failures

### 1. WeWork

**What happened:** $47B valuation → Chapter 11 bankruptcy.

**Fatal assumptions:**
- Could scale real estate like software
- Growth would solve profitability
- Valuation based on narrative, not fundamentals

**What went wrong:**
- Long-term leases, short-term revenue = cash flow mismatch
- Governance failure — CEO treated as personal piggy bank
- Aggressive expansion > demand
- COVID exposed fragile model

**What it teaches:**
- Cash flow discipline is non-negotiable
- Governance matters
- Growth without profit is a trap

---

### 2. Theranos

**What happened:** $9B valuation → criminal charges, company dissolved.

**Fatal assumptions:**
- Technology worked as claimed
- Could hide reality from investors/regulators
- Hype = value

**What went wrong:**
- Technology didn't work
- Fraudulent practices exposed
- No revenue, no product, just claims

**What it teaches:**
- Fake it till you make it doesn't work in hardware
- Regulation exists for reason
- Due diligence matters

---

### 3. Wirecard

**What happened:** $4B company → insolvency due to $1.9B fraud.

**Fatal assumptions:**
- Could hide losses indefinitely
- Auditors wouldn't find issues
- Growth was real

**What went wrong:**
- Fabricated €1.9B in profits
- Exposed by auditors
- No actual business behind numbers

**What it teaches:**
- Financial controls are essential
- Auditing isn't optional
- Eventually, numbers must be real

---

### 4. General Startup Failures (29% of startups)

**What happened:** Run out of cash.

**Fatal assumptions:**
- Funding would always be available
- Revenue would grow faster than expenses
- Could figure it out later

**What went wrong:**
- Cash flow mismatch
- Burn > revenue
- No runway visibility

**What it teaches:**
- Cash is oxygen
- Runway math matters
- Plan for adversity

---

### 5. General Corporate Examples (Kodak, Blockbuster, etc.)

**What happened:** Market leaders → irrelevance.

**Fatal assumptions:**
- Current business would last forever
- New technology is a threat to be managed
- They had time to adapt

**What went wrong:**
- Disrupted by new technology
- Failed to adapt
- Sat on cash instead of investing

**What it teaches:**
- Cash without innovation = slow death
- Disruption waits for no one
- Invest in the future, not the past

---

## Cross-Cutting Lessons

| Success Factors | Failure Factors |
|----------------|----------------|
| Long-term thinking | Short-term focus |
| Unit economics | Burn without path to profit |
| Cash discipline | Cash flow mismatch |
| Customer revenue | Funding dependent |
| Governance | Founder as piggy bank |
| Innovation investment | Complacency |

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Cash is oxygen** — 29% of failures from cash flow
2. **Profit ≠ cash** — Accrual vs. cash basis
3. **Unit economics first** — LTV:CAC 3:1+ before scaling
4. **Long-term > short-term** — Amazon's patience
5. **Governance matters** — WeWork's board failure
6. **Pricing is strategic** — Apple margin power
7. **VC is optional** — Bootstrapped success exists
8. **Retention > acquisition** — Churn compounds
