# Economics — Foundations

## Core Definition

Economics is the study of how agents (people, companies, governments) make decisions under scarcity. Understanding economics helps predict market behavior and make better business decisions.

---

## Core Concepts

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Supply and Demand** | Price determination |
| **Opportunity Cost** | Cost of next best alternative |
| **Elasticity** | Responsiveness to price changes |
| **Marginal Analysis** | Decision at the margin |
| **Incentives** | What drives behavior |
| **Scarcity** | Limited resources, unlimited wants |

---

## Market Types

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Perfect Competition** | Many buyers/sellers, identical products |
| **Monopolistic Competition** | Many sellers, differentiated products |
| **Oligopoly** | Few dominant sellers |
| **Monopoly** | Single seller |

---

## Key Principles

1. **Incentives matter** — People respond to incentives
2. **Think at the margin** — Decisions are incremental
3. **Opportunity cost is real** — Every choice has a cost
4. **Markets tend toward equilibrium**
5. **Unintended consequences exist**

---

## Business Applications

| Application | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Pricing** | Elasticity affects optimal price |
| **Strategy** | Market structure affects competition |

| **Risk** | Diversification reduces risk |
