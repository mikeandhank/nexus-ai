# Economics — Contrarian & Case Studies

## Contrarian

### What Most People Get Wrong

- **"Prices reflect value"** → Prices reflect supply and demand
- **"Rational actors"** → People make systematic errors
- **"Markets are efficient"** → Inefficiencies exist
- **"Competition is bad"** → Competition drives innovation
- **"More is always better"** → Diminishing returns apply

## 5 Successes

### 1. Amazon

**What happened:** Low prices, high volume.

**Core insight:**
- Economies of scale
- Price elasticity works
- Long-term thinking

### 2. Uber

**What happened:** Dynamic pricing works.

**Core insight:**
- Supply responds to price
- Surge pricing allocates scarce resources
- Real-time matching

### 3. Subscription Economy

**What happened:** Recurring revenue models.

**Core insight:**
- Lifetime value matters
- Churn has economic cost
- Predictable revenue

### 4. Airlines

**What happened:** Price discrimination at scale.

**Core insight:**
- Same product, different prices
- Willingness to pay varies
- Revenue management

### 5. Amazon Prime

**What happened:** Membership changes behavior.

**Core insight:**
- Membership changes incentives
- Commitment increases spending
- Network effects

## 5 Failures

### 1. Price Floors/Ceilings

**Government intervention → shortages/surpluses**

### 2. Rent Control

**Price caps → reduced supply, poor quality**

### 3. Monopolies

**No competition → higher prices, less innovation**

### 4. Information Asymmetry

**One side knows more → market failure**

### 5. Externalities

**Unaccounted costs → market inefficiency
