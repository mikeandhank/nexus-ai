# Agentic AI — Case Studies

## 5 Successes

### 1. Bank of America — Erica

**What happened:** AI assistant handling over 1 billion conversations.

**Core insight:**
- Started with narrow use case (financial guidance)
- Built trust through consistent, helpful responses
- Integrated into existing mobile banking

**What it teaches:**
- Start narrow, expand gradually
- Trust is built through reliability
- Integration beats innovation for adoption

---

### 2. JPMorgan Chase — Coach AI

**What happened:** Real-time AI tool for financial advisors.

**Core insight:**
- Augments human advisors, doesn't replace them
- Enables 95% faster response during market volatility
- High-stakes domain requires human-AI collaboration

**What it teaches:**
- High-stakes = human-in-the-loop
- Speed matters in time-sensitive domains
- Augmentation > automation in regulated industries

---

### 3. Mercedes-Benz Financial Services

**What happened:** Multi-agent CRM system implementation.

**Core insight:**
- Reduced customer complaints by 25%
- 20% growth in new business acquisitions
- 15% boost in cross-selling

**What it teaches:**
- Multi-agent can work for complex workflows
- Clear ROI metrics (complaints, acquisitions, cross-sell)
- Enterprise integration is achievable with proper planning

---

### 4. Credit Union Implementation

**What happened:** AI-powered customer service agent.

**Core insight:**
- 76% reduction in wait times
- 67% of questions handled without human intervention
- 240% ROI in first year

**What it teaches:**
- ROI can be proven quickly with right metrics
- Automation of routine = massive efficiency gain
- Customer service is low-hanging fruit

---

### 5. Salesforce — Agentforce

**What happened:** Enterprise agent platform with 18,000+ deals.

**Core insight:**
- Built on existing CRM data
- Integrates with enterprise workflows
- Clear pricing and implementation path

**What it teaches:**
- Leverage existing infrastructure
- Enterprise customers want proven platforms
- Distribution and integration matter more than technology

---

## 5 Failures / Issues

### 1. General Enterprise Deployments

**What happened:** 95% of corporate AI agent projects fail to reach production.

**Fatal assumption:**
- Pilot success = production readiness

**What went wrong:**
- Scaling exposes integration failures
- Cost overruns from real-world usage
- Governance gaps
- Data quality issues

**What it teaches:**
- Pilots are not proofs of production viability
- Plan for scaling from day one
- Governance is not optional

---

### 2. "Agent Washing"

**What happened:** Vendors rebrand existing tools as "agentic" without actual capabilities.

**Fatal assumption:**
- Marketing = capability

**What went wrong:**
- Customers buy, then discover limitations
- Only ~130 of thousands of "agentic" vendors have genuine capabilities
- Erodes trust in the category

**What it teaches:**
- Due diligence is essential
- Test capabilities, don't trust marketing
- The market will punish empty promises

---

### 3. Customer Trust Failures

**What happened:** Brands lose customers after emotional mishandlings.

**Fatal assumption:**
- Agents can handle all customer interactions

**What went wrong:**
- 72% of customers fear AI-only interactions
- Agents lack empathy for high-emotion situations
- Irreversible relationship damage

**What it teaches:**
- Not all interactions should be automated
- Emotional intelligence matters
- Human escalation is feature, not bug

---

### 4. Security Exploits

**What happened:** Cybercriminals exploit AI agents for scams.

**Fatal assumption:**
- Security is handled by the platform

**What went wrong:**
- Agents have broad access
- Non-human identity management is hard
- Attackers target the agent layer

**What it teaches:**
- Security must be designed in, not added on
- Least-privilege access for agents
- Monitoring and anomaly detection essential

---

### 5. Cost Overruns

**What happened:** Projects exceed budgets due to unpredictable costs.

**Fatal assumption:**
- Cost = API calls × model price

**What went wrong:**
- Recursive calls multiply costs
- Retries add significant expense
- Long context windows are expensive
- Real-world usage > demo costs

**What it teaches:**
- Model costs before deploying
- Implement cost guardrails
- Budget for operational monitoring
- "Cheap" agents can become expensive

---

## Cross-Cutting Lessons

| Success Factors | Failure Factors |
|----------------|----------------|
| Clear ROI metrics | Vague objectives |
| Phased rollout | Scaling too fast |
| Human-in-the-loop | Full automation ambition |
| Clean data | Poor data quality |
| Governance built-in | Governance as afterthought |
| Integration focus | Technology-first |
| Cost modeling | Cost surprise |

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Start narrow, expand gradually** — Don't try to automate everything at once
2. **ROI must be measurable** — Define success before starting
3. **Human-in-the-loop is feature** — Not a weakness
4. **Governance is critical** — 40% of failures are governance-related
5. **Integration is the bottleneck** — Technology is easy; integration is hard
6. **Cost modeling is essential** — Budget for reality, not demos
7. **Trust takes time** — Bank of America's billion conversations didn't happen overnight
